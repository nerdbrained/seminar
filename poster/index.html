Invariant Theory

18:30

by Brennen Creighton-Young

Symmetries naturally arise throughout the natural sciences, from particle physics to organic chemistry. Symmetries of these sorts can be better understood by using the tools of group theory, particularly group actions, to capture any underlying structure that may be present. It may seem natural to study how group actions behave with polynomials - but this turns out to be rather unenlightening.
